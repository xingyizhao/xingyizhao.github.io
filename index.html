Xingyi Zhao

PhD Candidate, Utah State University • NLP & AI Security

I am Xingyi Zhao, a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Utah State University, advised by Dr. Shuhan Yuan. I received both my B.E. and M.E. from the School of Artificial Intelligence and Automation, Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST). My research focuses on LLM security and safe post-training, with interests in faithful reasoning, red-teaming, and alignment-oriented training methods (e.g., SFT and RLHF). I study how malicious behaviors emerge and persist in language models, and I develop practical methods for malicious alignment analysis, harmful information unlearning, and safety post-training while preserving model utility.
